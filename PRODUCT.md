# Empowered Healing Counseling

## Who this is for

People in Washington state looking for a therapist who works with the body, not just the talk. Specifically:

- Adults navigating trauma, anxiety, mood disorders (including perinatal), OCD, grief
- Couples wanting to stop the same fight on loop
- Parents seeking play therapy for a child
- People healing from diet culture and disordered eating
- Folks who explicitly want a queer-affirming, neurodivergent-affirming, BIPOC-safe, body-positive therapist
- Tacoma / South Sound locals (in-person + Washington telehealth)

The visitor is usually anxious about the choice. They're scanning fast for "can I trust this person."

## Product purpose

The site is the introduction. It should make a stranger feel safe enough to schedule a 15-minute consult. Not "convert leads." Build trust, signal craft, name the work plainly.

## Voice

Meaghan writes in first person, with warmth and conviction. She names her own positionality (white, queer, neurodivergent, larger body, PCOS). She uses clinical language when it's the right word and plain language when that lands harder. She is anti-diet, anti-oppression, trauma-informed, body-aware. She works with Somatic Experiencing — the body is part of the room.

**Tone words:** grounded, embodied, plain-spoken, warm without sweetness, clinically literate, relational.

**Not:** corporate-wellness, motivational-poster, lavender-and-eucalyptus spa, "elevate your journey."

## Anti-references

Reject the saturated therapist-website aesthetic:

- Sage green + cream + a single stock photo of cupped hands holding a candle
- Soft-pink-and-terracotta wellness-startup template
- Navy-and-gold "executive coaching" register
- Generic "healing journey" gradients and pulsing dot CTAs
- 3D-rotated buttons, perspective hover effects, rotating radial glows
- Identical 3- or 4-card "what we offer" grids
- Centered hero with subtitle and two buttons

## Strategic principles

1. **The body is in the room.** Somatic Experiencing is the through-line. Visuals should acknowledge embodiment without resorting to anatomical or new-age clichés.
2. **Plain trust signals over polish theater.** License number, location, modalities, accepted insurance — visible and unfussy.
3. **Single voice, first person.** Meaghan is the practice. No "we deliver" copy.
4. **Editorial, not corporate.** Print-magazine layout instincts beat SaaS-landing-page instincts.
5. **Queer/neurodivergent affirming is explicit, not subtext.** The brand says it because the work is it.
6. **Pacific Northwest location is a flavor, not a stamp.** Don't lean on Tacoma stock photos.
